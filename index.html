Travelling: Exploring Nature's Hilly

I love traveling to hilly areas where I can immerse myself in the local culture and interact with the people. These destinations offer not only breathtaking landscapes but also vibrant traditions and customs. Whether it's exploring traditional crafts in remote villages, participating in local festivals, or enjoying the hospitality of communities with rich cultural heritage, these trips provide a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural depth. Engaging with locals and experiencing their way of life adds a meaningful dimension to the journey, making every trip both memorable and enriching.

The opportunity to engage with locals, whether through sharing meals, staying in homestays, or joining in communal activities, enriches my travel experience. Each journey becomes a blend of breathtaking natural beauty and deep cultural connection, making these travels both memorable and profoundly meaningful.

Kodaikanal

I absolutely love Kodaikanal for its breathtaking landscapes and serene climate. The tranquil beauty of Kodaikanal Lake and the panoramic views from Coaker’s Walk make it a perfect escape. The blend of lush greenery, cool weather, and charming local culture creates a truly magical experience.

Coaker’s Walk

Enjoy a mystic walk among the clouds at the Coakers walk, near Kodaikanal Lake. Nature enthusiasts and shutterbugs should not miss the place, for the view and ambience it offers are indescribable.

Kurinji Andavar Temple

Lord Muruga beckons you to come to Kurinji Andavar for he is the presiding deity, also called God of Hills. The temple is popular not only with devotees but, also with tourists that come here expecting to see the rare Kurinji flower.

Pine Forest

Pine Forests are cloaked in a rustic charm, and walking under the canopy of these old pine trees will offer much-needed solitude for those seeking it.
